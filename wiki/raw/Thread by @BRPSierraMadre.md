One ship. Multiple identities. That’s not a glitch — that’s a tactic. A Chinese dredger operating in Philippine waters was reportedly tracked using over 30 different identities, manipulating the Automatic Identification System (AIS) — a system designed for transparency and safety at sea. By constantly changing its identity, the vessel becomes harder to track, harder to monitor, and harder to hold accountable. Operating in areas like Manila Bay and Zambales under shifting identities raises a serious question: why hide in plain sight? This isn’t routine maritime activity — it’s deliberate evasion. It suggests intent to bypass regulations, avoid scrutiny, and operate without clear accountability.  
一艘船，多個身分。這不是系統故障，而是一種策略。據報道，一艘在菲律賓水域作業的中國挖泥船使用了超過 30 個不同的身份，操縱了自動識別系統（AIS）——該系統旨在提高海上透明度和安全性。透過不斷變換身份，這艘船更難被追蹤、監控和追究責任。在馬尼拉灣和讚巴萊斯等海域以不斷變化的身份作業，引發了一個嚴重的問題：為什麼要明目張膽地隱藏自己？這並非例行海上作業，而是蓄意規避。這表明其意圖是繞過法規、逃避審查，並在缺乏明確問責機制的情況下作業。  
  
Under UNCLOS, maritime activities are governed by clear rules — transparency, lawful conduct, and respect for coastal state rights within their Exclusive Economic Zone. The 2016 Arbitral Award further affirmed these rights in the West Philippine Sea, rejecting expansive and unsupported claims. Ongoing discussions on a Code of Conduct in the South China Sea are meant to reinforce these principles — not to enable actions that undermine them.  
根據《聯合國海洋法公約》，海上活動受明確規則約束——透明、合法行為以及尊重沿海國在其專屬經濟區內的權利。 2016年仲裁裁決進一步確認了西菲律賓海的這些權利，駁回了範圍過廣且缺乏依據的主張。目前正在進行的南海行為準則討論旨在強化這些原則，而非助長破壞這些原則的行為。
